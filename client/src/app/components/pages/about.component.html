JioMakan

At Jiomakan, we believe that great conversations and new friendships are best made over a delicious meal. Our platform connects food lovers with others who share similar interests, creating opportunities to dine together, chat about what you love, and build lasting connections. Whether you're a foodie looking for a new dining buddy or someone wanting to bond over shared topics, Jiomakan is the place where strangers turn into friends, one meal at a time.

Jio /dʒiːoʊ/

A colloquial term used in Southeast Asia, specifically in Malaysia and Singapore, referring to an invitation or gathering, often with friends or family, to socialize, share meals, or enjoy experiences together.

Example

"Wahh why just now you go makan never jio me!"

Etymology:

Derived from the Malay word for "invite," often used in the context of casual and friendly gatherings.

Makan /ˈma.kan/

A Malay word meaning "to eat" or "meal." It is commonly used in Southeast Asia, particularly in Malaysia and Singapore, to refer to eating or dining in a casual context, often with family or friends.

Example

"Paiseh lah, next time I jio you makan ok?"

Etymology:

The word "makan" comes from the Malay language, where it is the verb for "to eat."
